<div class="join-callout">
  <span class="join-label">&#x2022; Now Recruiting</span>
  <p>I am always looking for motivated students and talented postdocs to work with me and colleagues in the <a href="http://www.dcc.uchile.cl/">Department of Computer Science</a> at the University of Chile. <strong>Right now I am looking for students to start from April 2026.</strong></p>
</div>

<div class="join-section">
  <h2>Graduate Students (MSc &amp; PhD)</h2>
  <p>Your research interests should align with one or more of the following areas:</p>
  <ul>
    <li>Algorithms &amp; society — fairness, accountability, and societal impact of algorithmic systems</li>
    <li>Computational social science — modeling human behavior at scale</li>
    <li>Machine learning — interpretable models, decision trees, LLM-based methods</li>
    <li>Social computing — online experiments, recommendation systems, collective behavior</li>
  </ul>
  <p>Interested students should email me with a brief statement of your research interests and how they align with the <a href="/publications/">current and previous research</a> in the lab. Please include your CV and any relevant academic transcripts.</p>
  <p>Our department offers full-time research <strong>MSc and PhD programs</strong> and students can usually obtain funding through an <strong>ANID graduate fellowship</strong>. Depending on your academic record, we can also explore a broader range of funding options for MSc students.</p>
  <p style="margin-top:1.2em;">
    <a href="mailto:aabeliuk@uchile.cl" class="btn btn--primary">Email me to apply</a>
  </p>
</div>

<div class="join-section">
  <h2>Postdoctoral Researchers</h2>
  <p>If you are looking for postdoc opportunities and have a strong background in <strong>machine learning, computational social science, or algorithmic fairness</strong>, I would love to hear from you.</p>
  <p>I may not have direct funding for a position, but we can write a postdoc research proposal together for <strong>ANID</strong> (Chile's national research agency). The funding rate is competitive compared to US and European-based grants.</p>

  <div class="join-timeline-note">
    <strong>ANID timeline:</strong> The call opens January–March with a deadline at the end of March. The position would start approximately one year after the award. Please take this timeline into account — we should begin working on a proposal <strong>at least one year before your planned arrival</strong>, given ANID's long review process.
